The developer interviewed one of Travelport’s Atlas design team members, a senior product designer, Elaine O’Neill. The developer conducted this interview to gather more information about the Atlas Design System from a designer who has been with Travelport since the early stages of Atlas. The developer will summarise the information collected from the interview below. The documents of this interview can be found in Appendix C.

Getting Started in Atlas

Elaine, now senior product designer for the Atlas design system team, first joined the team after completing a master's degree in 2019 and started as an associate product designer for Atlas. She mentioned that this role was her first job in product design and stated that it was a learning curve. She explained the motivation behind implementing a design system for Travelport. The company has a variety of applications, websites, and products that previously did not look and feel like the Travelport brand. Travelport's designers created components and patterns individually, leading to inconsistency, time-wasting, and more effort needed in development and prototyping. Since Atlas, there has been improved consistency throughout the organisation's products.

The Benefits of a Design System

Elaine then listed the many benefits of other teams in Travelport using the Atlas design system. One key advantage was on the design end; the designers can focus more on innovation, research, and user experience. They understand that by using Atlas, their components are consistent across all products. By following guidelines on the components, patterns, and more, the designers can produce designs that lead from the latest best practices in UX/UI design. She then explained more critical benefits for the developers, the company, and branding.

The Challenges of a Design System

When asked if Elaine faced any challenges throughout being a designer on the Atlas design team, Elaine explained how communication and collaboration outside of the core team is a constant challenge. It is challenging to receive feedback, requirements, and input from other designers outside of the core team, but she stated that it is a necessary task to complete. Another challenge she mentioned was it is difficult for some products to adopt the Atlas design system. This challenge is due to these products having complex user interfaces and technology debt.

Lastly, Elaine was asked about designers outside Atlas that she would recommend. She mentioned two designers, Brad Frost and Nathan Curtis, who she references a lot regarding best practices in design systems. Brad Frost is the author of Atomic Design, which discusses designing systems of components. She also said a few more designers and podcasts about the challenges designers face in design systems.
